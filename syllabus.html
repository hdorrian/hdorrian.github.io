Number

  • Understand and use decimals and measures of any size, as well as use the symbols (=, ≠, <, >, ≤, ≥), brackets, powers, roots and reciprocals
  • Be able to use prime numbers, factors and multiples, common factors, common multiples, prime factorisation
  • Use mass, length, time, money and other measures, including with decimal quantities
  • Round numbers and use significant figures
  • Use a calculator!

Algebra

  • Use and interpret algebraic notation, including:
    1. ab in place of a × b
    2. 3y in place of y + y + y and 3 × y
    3. a^2 in place of a × a, a^3 in place of a × a × a
    4. a^2b in place of a × a × b
    5. a/b in place of a ÷ b
  • Use fractions rather than as decimals
  • Rearrange formulae to change the subject
  • Be able to use graphs!
  • Calculate and understand gradients and intercepts of graphs
  • Use linear and quadratic graphs to estimate values of y for given values of x and vice versa

Ratio, proportion and rates of change

  • Change between standard units (for example time, length)
  • Use scale factors, scale diagrams and maps
  • Use ratio notation, including reduction to simplest form
  • Solve problems involving percentage change, including percentage increase and decrease
  • Solve problems involving direct and inverse proportion, including graphical and algebraic representations

Probability

  • Record, describe and analyse the frequency of outcomes of simple probability
  • Understand that the probabilities of all possible outcomes sum to 1
  • Generate theoretical sample spaces for single and combined events with equally likely, mutually exclusive outcomes and use these to calculate theoretical probabilities

Statistics

  • Describe, interpret and compare observed distributions of a single variable through graphs involving discrete, continuous and grouped data
  • Construct and interpret appropriate tables, charts, and diagrams
  • Describe simple mathematical relationships between two variables
